Bitwise Operators

Bitwise operators perform operations on numbers at the binary level. In JavaScript, they convert operands to 32-bit signed integers and manipulate them as a sequence of bits (0s and 1s).

These operators are commonly found in lower-level programming tasks or specific domains like data compression, encryption, or optimization algorithms.

List of Bitwise Operators

  • AND (&)
  • OR (|)
  • XOR (^)
  • NOT (~)
  • LEFT SHIFT (<<)
  • RIGHT SHIFT (>>)
  • ZERO-FILL RIGHT SHIFT (>>>)

Bitwise operations are rarely needed in everyday web development but can be powerful tools in specific technical domains. If you ever dive into fields like cryptography or systems-level optimization, these operators become valuable.
